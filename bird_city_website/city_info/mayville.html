1.1.2, Document that current community planning seeks to provide additional bird habitat

There are several parks located in the City of Mayville. Bird houses have been put up in all of the parks, whenever possible, in hopes of attracting birds. Mayville’s outdoor swimming pool was previously closed and is now a natural pond with aeration pumps. The pond has been attracting some migratory birds including a couple of Sandhill Cranes. Bird houses are abundant in the parks and the new natural pond.

1.4.1, Show that your community offers the public information on how they can control and remove invasive species in order to improve or maintain bird habitat

Invasive plant species are plants that are not native to this area that have either been intentionally introduced or have escaped cultivation. These plants threaten the ecological balance of our natural lands by displacing native plant species. These plants lack the predators and diseases that keep them in balance in their native habitats. The Mayville Area Chamber of Commerce has developed and printed an Invasive Species Brochure. This brochure is in both printed and electronic format. Printed brochures are available at the Mayville Chamber of Commerce and Mayville City Hall. The brochure is also available electronically on the City of Mayville's website.

The City of Mayville celebrates International Migratory Bird Day in conjunction with the annual Horicon Marsh Bird Festival. In 2022, the Mayville Common Council designated May 7, 2022 as IMBD, and the Bird Festival was held May 6, 2022-May 8, 2022.  The Mayville Common Council intends to designate May 13, 2023 as IMBD, the resolution will be presented at the February 13, 2023 Common Council Meeting.
